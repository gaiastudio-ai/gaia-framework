# Clarification Notes

> **Closing-artifact bias:** `clarification-notes` (mode: `clarify`).
> Drafted at CLOSE — feeds the facilitator's notes-drafting prompt.
>
> The `clarify` meeting exists to **explain a topic for the user**, not to
> decide anything and not to brainstorm new ideas. These notes capture the
> questions raised and the expert answers given — nothing more. If the
> discussion drifts toward picking an option, that belongs in a `decide`
> meeting; if it drifts toward generating new ideas, that belongs in a
> `brainstorm` meeting.

## Questions asked

List the question(s) the user brought to the meeting, verbatim where possible.

- Question 1 — what the user wanted clarified.
- Question 2 — what the user wanted clarified.

## Expert answers

For each question, capture the answers and explanations the invited experts
gave, attributed by name. Keep these explanatory — the goal is understanding,
not a recommendation.

- **{Expert name}** — answer / explanation for question 1.
- **{Expert name}** — answer / explanation for question 2.

## Agreement and disagreement

Where the experts agreed or disagreed **on the explanation itself** (e.g. the
mechanism, the cause, the meaning of a term) — not on what to do about it.

- Agreement — point the experts converged on.
- Disagreement — point the experts saw differently, and why.

## Open follow-up questions

Questions that remained unresolved after the discussion — candidates for a
follow-up `clarify-followup` action item or a separate meeting.

- Open question 1 — what still needs clarifying.
- Open question 2 — what still needs clarifying.
